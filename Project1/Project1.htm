Website Critiques

Questions:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Website 1 Critique - Trader Joe's

www.traderjoes.com

  1. My first impression is good, I like the aesthetic, it is in accordance with the brand and has a simple layout.
  2. The main page has featured items, receipes and events. The page feels simple enough that I can find anything I need, but not boring.
    It has a scrolling feature so there are the lists with the most relevant information,
    and nagivation bars at the top for other more specific things a customer may be looking for.
    The features lower on the page seem a little random, but the aesthetic is good and the information all makes sense.
  3. The structure makes sense, it flows well and I am able to navigate it intuitively.
    There are tabs at the top for some common things people would want to view on their site (like career opportunities, announcements, stores etc.).
    And there is a hamburger menu to the left with more links to current grocery items and lots of other information.
  4. The graphics are nice, with little ilustrations in thier brand's style, designed border around the images at the top, and pictures of their food.
    The images look like they were kind of randomly taken (not in a studio for advertising food, it looks like a phone just took it)
    which is a bit odd, but it could be stylistic to their homey vibe.
  5. The page loaded almost immediately, it was very reasonable and the nagivation is fast as well.
  6. The text sizing is good for the titles at the top, but lower down they are kind of small and it gives the site
    a strange flow (it starts to just feel like a lot of lists).
    Having an image at the top enhances the site, but the choice of image could have been better.
    There is a background color for the top section, which is a nice addition. I wish they had more background colors down the page,
    the rest of the page is mostly a white background which leaves the page feeling a little cheap or unprofessional.
  7. The tabs at the top are very simplistic, and the hamburger menu on the left is really useful. It is easy to naviagte but could be more stylistic.
    I find the lists below the introduction of the page to be a little random, those could've just been in the tabs under "Announcements" or "Events"
    The hamburger menu has a lot of options for a small grocery store like Trader Joes, but I think they are all nice.
    I would've combined some of the options to make it less chaotic.
  8. The main page holds to the color scheme of Trader Joe's as a brand very well, but the links to the grocery items ended up falling flat.
    They are mainly just a white background with plain text (but a matching font), and pictures of their food.
    It was very plain- which seems kind of strange since thier groceries are why people know Trader Joe's as a brand.
    They could have included more of their signature Trader Joe's Red color scheme with the black illustration-style pictures all around the page.
    I do think the left panel has some nice filters and tags with a nice peachy brown color that suits their style.
    Overall it looks really nice and keept to the Trader Joe's simplistic, quirky, style.
  9. The site needs to be full sized to see a lot of the style, or the left banner with "recipes" and other options relating to the groceries, on the grocery pages.
    The page adjusts slightly to a smaller window, but it does lose functionality because you must scroll around a lot to find the options for filtering.
  10. This site offers a lot of interaction with their products (recipes, ways to pair them etc.) and events and things that I didn't know this store even had.
    I like that this page has a lot of extra features, and that it is really naviagable.
    They sacrificed some of the easy functionality for having more information on their site that some may find unecessary, but I think it was worthwhile.
    Most people who visit a Trader Joe's site are likely looking for recipes, checking the current products, or events because they love the brand,
    those who won't appreciate these additions are likely not checking the site anyways and just shop in person.
    They know their audience and offer a Trader Joe's culture through their website.
